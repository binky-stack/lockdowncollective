View From a Chelsea Flat: 1987

by Beverly Frydman

The view from the bathroom window soothed me
much better than mugs of chamomile tea
Those terracotta chimney pots stood tall
under lingering clouds with nowhere to be

The chimneys did not blow smoke, they were still
Sat on stacks of mismatched bricks and pointed roofs
shingled in slate, they crowned the terraced houses
former workmanâ€™s cottages in Chelsea

Knelt on the avocado toilet seat
I pushed the sash window open a crack
breathed in starless sky and a memory
that did not belong to me: smoke filled nights

I put that souvenir in my pocket
for another time, so I could bathe my
thoughts in darkness undisturbed until
the milk man delivered my morning
