Process

by Beverly Frydman

I fill pages with all the words I know
scribble on walls from ceiling to floor
I write so hard the paper burns away
and the walls collapse from the weight of me

Single letters fly out from the debris
arrange themselves in clouds above my head
if I’m lucky words drop into my hands
so I can feel them see them drink them down

and I let them swim around for a while
through my eardrums, heartbeat and pinkie toes
my lungs take them in and when they’re released
the words self- publish themselves on my skin
