Coca Cola & Cups of Tea

by Beverly Frydman

On the train from New York to New Orleans
my grandmother and child father were free
to be themselves on the way to her hometown.
They only drank Coca Cola and ate potato chips

At Heathrow on my way back to New York
I wrapped myself in wooly airport time,
loaded up on flat whites and salt and vinegar crisps.
There is no reason to travel light

Now we lock down, shelter at home
reminding each other of what day it is
I start planning dinner at breakfast
and countdown to gin & tonic at 4

We go out for our one-hour walk
no cars or planes or drunken singing
or kids laughing their way home from school
we wave to neighbours from a distance

Outside the birds eat worms, sing loudly
we flick on the kettle for more cups of tea
