Poodle

by Beverly Frydman

My nightmares used to live
inside an empty wine bottle
covered by a pink knitted poodle
made by someone else’s grandma

After my parents went to bed
and the hallway light was off
the poodle spoke to me
not in human or dog language

It could only squeak and buzz
because it had no tongue.
Its mouth, a stitched black line
that could not open

Tiny silver glass eyes
that never shut
saw me hiding under
the pink satin bedspread

It sat high on the bookshelf
next to Alice in Wonderland
and Grimm’s Fairy Tales.
The poodle knew things

I finally screamed
to drown out the sounds
to make my mother come in
and take the poodle away

and turn the hallway light on
and to tell me, to tell me
it’s alright, yes it’s alright,
my baby, don’t think twice
