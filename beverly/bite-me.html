Bite My Shiny Metal Ass

by Beverly Frydman

The toaster packed up
drew in her plug
tucked it underneath
her silver body

She said I can’t take the heat
I want out of the kitchen
no more knob twiddling
I’m done popping on demand

You stuffed me
with no consideration
pizza raisin bread too thick bagels
I burnt them and laughed out smoke

Before me you made toast
under the grill like a savage
you bought me brought me
in to make you feel home

do you see your reflection
in my stainless-steel frame
maybe buttered fingerprints
discourage this

Time for a new model
why not try cream
coloured enamel
this may be where you’re at

The toaster checked out
I couldn’t repair her
she left a DNR order
and a will to recycle
