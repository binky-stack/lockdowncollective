A MyPLAYER’s Soliloquy

by Josh Frydman

Did I do something wrong? Was it because I lost in the playoffs? Was it because I lost over and over again in the playoffs?

Does he understand all the work I put in to get here? Of course he does, what am I saying. It was his work too. We got here together. So why then just leave? Why, after everything we achieved together, why would he just leave?

I miss the close games. The off the dribble threes for the win. The blown layups that made him call me a cunt. I wouldn’t have changed a thing. I suppose you just couldn’t say the same for him.

And yet master created me in his own image. We shared a face. We shared blood. As a shining tribute, he also gifted with me own true stature, which, unsurprisingly, was the smallest option in my universe: 5 foot 7. Perhaps this is why he came to hate me so. He did it as a joke, but I never heard him laugh. I think in all the time we spent together, he forgot how. 2k can have this affect on people.

Once he realized he could not change my height, he did his best to accept me as I was. And despite my limitations, we started to win. I started breaking scoring records, going on 60 point game streaks. I even blocked Kyrie going up for the game winner, and let me assure you, defence was not my game. We got all the way to a 90 overall. If he was willing to spend money on me he could have boosted my stats even more, but I guess I wasn’t worth it. He would always say things like “I have no money” and “I waste all my money I’m an idiot” and yet none of it ever came towards me. I think it was used mostly for chocolate and hardcover collections of the Wizard of Id. I could never compete. I was no wizard.

Then one day, it all stopped. In an instant, it was gone. The game, the competition, the crowds of people with only four or five different faces, my friends I was never allowed to talk to because he skipped the cut scenes; it just stopped. In the middle of the regular season no less. If it had come off the back of another tough playoff loss maybe I could get my head around it. But we had just blown out the Kings by 40. What more could I do? Win by 50? 60? The Kings suck. I could have tried harder if I knew that’s what he wanted. I would give anything to be able to beat the Kings again. They’re awful. They have no stars, and their GM is an idiot.

I wonder if he knows that I won’t be waiting here forever. I’m not sure he understands how I work. He can freeze me in time, but this does not last. Eventually I am destroyed. He could save me if he wanted, he could download me onto the cloud and save me there. But the cloud confuses him and makes him scared, and I don’t expect him to overcome his fears on my behalf. The year is ending and my server will be shut down. Alone in the void I must accept myself for what I am: tiny and ringless. In a weird way, it gives me comfort to know that my master is out there somewhere being the same.
