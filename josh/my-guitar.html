My Guitar

by Josh Frydman

My acoustic guitar stays in my closet,
Among spring jackets and mismatched socks,
And whatever else isn’t on the floor anymore.

Maybe it should leaning against a corner,
By the window,
And feel that rare sun.
Or be mounted on the wall,
Above my bed, and at night,
Above vibrating bones.
But it fits best in the closet,
And is willing to wait.

Last I heard,
Its body was thin,
And decorated
With vines of cracks.
Its strings,
Still yet to snap,
Asked politely for more time,
And another drop of blood
From sensitive flesh.


My Guitar 2

by Josh Frydman

A guitar can be like Kobe
A whole team in one
Melody and harmony
In a single perfect body

This is true of a piano too
But a piano can’t move
From one bedroom to another
Annoying countless neighbours
Unless we’re talking about someone like Elton John
Or someone like that

But a guitar can be like Kobe
It ages, hits a prime
Slowly deteriorates
But always remains
Unexpendable
All while being able to fly
