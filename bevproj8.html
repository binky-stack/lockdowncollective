Questions

by Beverly Frydman

Walked in woods with Angie who’s nearly 90
she was in an aqua track suit and a cardie
I wore a flowery dress and nettles stung my legs
Angie said to look for healing dock leaves
but I didn’t know what they looked like
she asked where you were raised

Ran in the big scrubs with Macy
stopped for a distanced conversation
with a bald man covered in tattoos
he was drawing tower blocks in charcoal
he was a bus driver he hated Andy Warhol
he asked where you are from

When I was 13 I went to Jill Coralnick’s house
to sit in her immaculate bedroom
and listen to Queen’s Night at the Opera
she told me that her parents were always angry
when I met them they had some questions
like where are your people from

I told my parents how I answered
my grandparents came from the Bronx
with Yiddish accents and brown paper shopping bags
filled with boxes of underwear from the lower east side
my parents laughed for hours, I did too
not really understanding any of it
