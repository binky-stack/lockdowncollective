August 17 th 8:30 am

by Beverly Frydman

An ironing board sits on the pavement. It is not broken. A runner passes me on the cross walk. His pink crop top shouts Bimbo. I follow him into the park. Empty crisp packets and burger wrappers are scattered across the grass. A bag of sliced white bread sits next to a jar of frankfurters on a sun-bleached bench. Twin girls in long red dresses jump off a log singing corona virus corona virus. A horse pulls a man in a cart and all the dogs follow. I wave them on.
