Conservation Grasses: Little Wormwood Scrubs

by Beverly Frydman

Stalks of mature grass
don’t want man handling
they’ll scratch till you bleed

seed-heads like coronets
dowager duchesses look like hay
ready for the harvest

they huddle in the middle of the lawn
surrounded by common blades
waiting to be cut down to size

young leaves are easy to pluck
suck their sweet roots spit them out
like they were nothing

old conservation grass stays put
refusing mowing and pruning
going to seed at a prickly pace
